---
title: "Check"
description: "A comparison of newly submitted business and bank details against a verification fingerprint to detect whether stored payee data may be compromised."
---

A **check** runs against an existing [verification](/objects/verification). You supply current business and bank account fields; ezyshield compares them to the verification’s stored fingerprint and returns whether the data appears **uncompromised** or **compromised** relative to that snapshot.

## Relationship to verifications

1. You [create a verification](/api-reference/verifications/create-a-verification) and drive it through the verification flow (including contact steps described on the [verification](/objects/verification) page).
2. Later—before a payout, on a schedule, or after a data change in your system—you [check a verification](/api-reference/checks/check-a-verification) by posting the details you hold **now**.
3. Each check is a separate record you can [list](/api-reference/checks/list-all-checks-for-a-verification) or [retrieve](/api-reference/checks/get-a-check-for-a-verification).

Checks do not replace a full verification when onboarding a payee; they answer “do today’s details still align with the verified fingerprint?”

## When to use checks

- **Before payments:** confirm bank and identity details have not drifted or been tampered with since verification.
- **Periodic reviews:** re-check high-value or long-lived payees on a cadence.
- **After internal updates:** your CRM or banking file changes should be checked against the last good verification.

## Testing bank details

In non-production environments, certain BSB codes simulate name-match and account outcomes. Use the table on [Test bank accounts](/testing/test-bank-accounts) so your integration exercises success and failure paths without affecting real banking data.

## API surface

All check endpoints are nested under a verification:

- [List all checks for a verification](/api-reference/checks/list-all-checks-for-a-verification)
- [Check a verification](/api-reference/checks/check-a-verification) (create a check)
- [Get a check for a verification](/api-reference/checks/get-a-check-for-a-verification)

See [Authentication](/api-reference/authentication) for required abilities (`check:read`, `check:write` per your API key).
